Covering the basics

Whether to Buy or Rent

Know about short & long term impact on you and your finances. Use a calculator to get an estimate. Also include factors:

  • Diversification and liquidity of assets
  • Potential tax savings and liens on purchase
  • Appreciation factors and house updates
  • Ongoing costs, taxes of home ownership
Your Financial Readiness

Includes more than having a strong income. You need to evaluate:

  • Your credit worthiness
  • Ability to put money down on the house and handle closing costs
  • The documentation you can provide to support your loan application
  • Pre-approval affects whether you are viewed as a strong buyer during negotiations
Decide on Type of Home You Want

Often involves compromises. It’s usual not to get the perfect home in the perfect location. Issues you’ll need to address:

  • Style of home suited for your family
  • Variance in Property taxes, insurance and HOA costs
  • New construction, ready to move-in, fixer upper
  • Will you need yard space, open kitchen, garage, etc
Your Preferred Location

Will have a big impact on your lifestyle. You can start with general location and then evaluate micro neighborhoods which meets your family preferences. Consider:

  • Schools and ratings. Neighborhood culture
  • Commute to work, shopping, amenities nearby
  • Evaluate location during night and day times. Get to know at street level, talk to residents
  • Local real estate trends and developments.
Mortgage You’d Prefer

With variety of available loans and terms, you would need to review these before talking to loan officer and make decision.
Start out by understanding:

  • Bank loans vs. independent mortgage lenders
  • Length of loan, terms (Fixed, ARM), types (FHA/Jumbo) and Interest Rates
  • Varied Down payment requirements
Selecting Real Estate Agent

An agent who gives you full attention, can help by pointing vital flaws in house, provide right valuation, negotiating for best terms, competitive with offers

  • Evaluate not only by star rating or sales number. Know their strengths and what works best for your needs
  • Interview few agents & do negotiate for value additions, cash backs or credits
  • Referral or online agents? Do you need as much or too less of their involvement?
Getting right services

You’ll need several different experts as you move through the home-buying journey. You will need to get right services at right time

  • Generally avoid houses which has structural issue (ex: shifted foundation). Other issues can be updated or fixed. Get a fair estimate from trusted contractor or handyman
  • Major cost hit for house repair comes from roof, plumbing, flooring, HVAC make sure to cover them
  • Get your own house inspection done from unbiased sources. Remember to ask termite report, county approval records for any extra units
Scene in Silicon valley

With Real Estate prices peaking new highs in SF bay area, it is natural for first time buyers to be intimidated or be in denial

  • Good practise not to bid too high just to get a piece of land in valley. Is there fundamentals to justify the price?
  • Does the market continue to boom or waiting to bust? There could be micro level cycles, do some research
  • Drive until you qualify! Horizontal or vertical development which one is for you?
New tech in real estate

Realty market is trending with apps and tools to make buying process effective and easier. Try below to be an early adopter

  • DIY: You can get all of buyers agent commission (up to 3%) to your pocket by self representing
  • Semi agent involvement: Do property search, evaluate and select. Involve real estate agent only during final offer stage
  • Get full service agents with cash back: Agents bid to give you highest money back
